Montebello Golf Course - Rick Smith Re-Design

Montebello Golf Course is undergoing a significant renovation led by golf course architect Rick Smith. The renovation began after the course closed on September 26, 2022. The course was previously 18 hole course designed by Max Behr with subsequent renovation work by David Rainville, ASGCA. The new course is a comprehensive rebuild effort and will feature 9 holes with a Topgolf driving range.

The updated course is scheduled to reopen with tee time availability in July or August, summer of 2024.

Rick Smith is a globally recognized golf course designer who has created several courses including Arcadia Bluffs by Lake Michigan, Wuskowhan Player's Club in Mount Olive, Michigan, R&S Sharf course at Oakland University in Rochester, Michigan, and the Shenandoah course at Turning Stone Resort in Verona, New York. The Shenandoah course has hosted a few PGA Tour events. Rick Smith is a design partner for Phil Mickelson Design Group.

Montebello Golf Course was built in 1928. The golf course is a city municipal golf course serving the community for almost 100 years. Montebello City Council approved a $60 million municipal bond in September 2022 to finance the project.
