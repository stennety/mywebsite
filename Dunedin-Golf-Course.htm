Dunedin - Donald Ross Restoration - Kris Spence

The comprehensive restoration project at Dunedin Golf Course is a hybrid of historical restoration with modern advancements, spearheaded by golf architect Kris Spence. Construction operations are being handled by XGD Systems, a leader in golf course construction.

A key part of the plan from Spence is peeling back the years of alterations to uncover the original greens designed by Donald Ross. Spence relates this to an archaeological dig, aimed at restoring the authentic characteristics of the greens by revealing Ross's original contours and strategic designs.

Central to the project is the restoration of 87 bunkers, meticulously returning them to Ross's original vision. This not only involves the revival of their strategic placement and aesthetic form but also aligns with the broader goal of restoring the course's historical integrity and strategic playability. Complementing the bunker restoration, the Dunedin project upgrades to modern turf science by introducing new grass varieties specifically chosen for their adaptability to the course's environment and play conditions. TifEagle Bermuda grass will be used for the greens. TifTuf Bermuda will be used for tees, fairways, and roughs. TifTuf is praised for its drought tolerance and durability.

A primary component of the restoration is the implementation of a new irrigation system provided by Toro installed by Wesco Turf Inc. This system features a 2-wire control and uses high-density polyethylene (HDPE) piping representing the latest in irrigation technology. This advanced system will ensure precise and efficient water management across the course by underpinning the sustainability of the newly renovated and planted areas. This modernization is essential for maintaining the course's condition and supporting the extensive renovations made to the tees, greens, fairways, and roughs.
